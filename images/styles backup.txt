About

Dr. Styles sass is originally from North Dakota and studied Literature at the University of Iowa. He did a study abroad semester at the University of the Basque Country in San Sebastian, where, having completed his Bachelor’s degree, he continued to teach English. Dr. Sass later went on to do a Masters and a Doctoral degree in American Studies at the Ruprecht-Karls-Universität in Heidelberg, writing on the campaign narratives of the 2008 US presidential election. He ran the Writing Center at the Heidelberg Center for American Studies and later was the Assistant Director of an American study abroad program. Dr. Sass currently lives in Stuttgart, where he is an instructor at AKAD University, and works as a writer, editor, and translator.

Originally from North Dakota, Styles Sass attended the University of Iowa from which, after completing his final semester abroad in the Basque Country, he received his Bachelor’s degree in English. Remaining in Northern Spain, he worked as a language trainer while pursuing his interests in literature and film. Moving to Germany, he was awarded several writing fellowships and published a collection of poetry and prose pieces titled, More Than These Few Days. His interest in the intersection of literature and politics brought him to the University of Heidelberg, where he completed a Master’s degree in American Studies.  He is currently a teaching assistant at the Heidelberg Center for American Studies, where he is doing his Ph.D. on the campaign narratives of the 2008 presidential election. He lives with his family in Stuttgart where he also works as a writer and editor.
